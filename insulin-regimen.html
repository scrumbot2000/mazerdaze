Sliding Scale — Check Blood Sugar Before Each Meal & At Bedtime

Add the sliding scale dose to your 3-unit mealtime dose at meals. At bedtime, give sliding scale dose only (no mealtime dose at bedtime).

Blood Sugar (mg/dL) Sliding Scale Dose At Meals: Total Dose At Bedtime: Dose
Under 200 0 units 3 units 0 units
200 – 249 1 unit 4 units 1 unit
250 – 299 2 units 5 units 2 units
300 – 349 3 units 6 units 3 units
350 – 399 4 units 7 units 4 units
400 – 449 5 units 8 units 5 units
450 or above ⚠️ Call your doctor's office. Do not dose without guidance.

Scale: 1 unit for every 50 mg/dL above 200. Target blood sugar: 200 mg/dL.

Important Reminders
  • Long-acting insulin (bedtime shot) is given every night regardless of blood sugar.
  • Short-acting insulin is given at meals only when eating. If skipping a meal, skip the mealtime dose — but still check your blood sugar and apply the sliding scale if needed.
  • Low blood sugar (under 70 mg/dL): Do NOT inject insulin. Treat with 4 oz juice or glucose tablets. Recheck in 15 minutes.
  • Sick days: Check blood sugar every 4 hours. Continue long-acting insulin. Call the office if blood sugar stays above 300 or you cannot eat.
  • Always check blood sugar before injecting. Never guess.
  • Keep a log of your readings and bring it to every appointment.
